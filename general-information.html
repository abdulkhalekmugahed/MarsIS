Curriculum Overview

Elementary and Middle School

At Mars International School (MarsIS), we proudly present a dynamic American curriculum designed to inspire innovation and learner agency in our classrooms. Rooted in unit and course designs, this curriculum is continually updated to prioritize learner outcomes. High-quality text materials, complemented by an exceptional library, enrich our teaching approach.

Mathematics

Aligned with the American Common Core State Standards, our math program shifts the focus from mere memorization to fostering a deep understanding. Students engage with mathematical concepts through models, pictures, and clear explanations, encouraging meaningful comprehension.

English Language Arts

Crafting Savvy Readers and Writers Following the American Common Core State Standards, our language arts program immerses students in meaningful activities. Emphasizing essential skills, students practice reading and writing across diverse texts, honing their ability to communicate with clarity and purpose.

Science

Guided by the NGSS Framework, our science education adopts a three-dimensional approach. Disciplinary Core Ideas, Crosscutting Concepts, and Engineering principles converge to challenge and inspire students. Beyond rote memorization, we cultivate curiosity and creativity, exploring connections across diverse science domains

Physical Education:

In alignment with SHAPE America standards, our physical education program provides a comprehensive, standards-based curriculum. Designed to develop motor skills, promote physical fitness, and instill sportsmanship, it emphasizes holistic well-being and emotional intelligence.

Music

Delivered through the Core Arts Standards, our music education program focuses on creating, performing, and responding. From refining musical compositions to instrumental and vocal performances, students explore the artistic realm of music.

Technology

Embracing technology as an integral part of daily school life, our approach is embedded in an integrated learning strategy. Technology becomes a tool for purposeful learning experiences, nurturing critical thinking skills and continuous inquiry

High School

MarsIS's high school educatinsttuon provides a selection of three educational programs: the college-preparatory program following the American curriculum, which encompasses more than 60 courses, including 10 courses at an advanced level; the program of Advanced Placement, which encompasses 20 courses recognized by the College Board; and the comprehensive International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma Programme, which encompasses 28 courses, including the subject of Theory of Knowledge (TOK), in addition to 12 certificate courses
